# Antibiotic Prescribing Guidelines
**Version:** 2024 | **Source:** NICE Antimicrobial Prescribing Guidelines / PHE START SMART THEN FOCUS | **Category:** prescribing, formulary, general

---

## 1. Core Principles of Antimicrobial Stewardship

- **Reserve antibiotics** for infections where there is clear clinical benefit
- **Take cultures before prescribing** in complex, recurrent, or serious infections
- **Use the narrowest spectrum** appropriate antibiotic
- **Prescribe the shortest effective duration** — longer courses increase resistance without improving outcomes
- **Document** indication, drug, dose, route, duration, and allergy status in all prescriptions
- **Review at 48–72 hours** (IV-to-oral switch; stop if cultures negative; de-escalate if sensitivities available)
- **No antibiotic** for viral upper respiratory tract infections, uncomplicated viral sore throats, or self-limiting viral illness

## 2. Urinary Tract Infections (UTI)

### 2.1 Uncomplicated Lower UTI — Women (non-pregnant)

**First-line:**
- **Nitrofurantoin** 100 mg modified-release BD for **5 days**
  - Avoid if eGFR <45 mL/min (inadequate urinary concentration; risk of peripheral neuropathy)

**Second-line (if nitrofurantoin contraindicated or resistant):**
- **Trimethoprim** 200 mg BD for **7 days**
  - Avoid if local resistance >20%, recent trimethoprim exposure, or eGFR <15 mL/min
  - Avoid in first trimester of pregnancy (folate antagonist)

**Third-line (pivmecillinam or fosfomycin — specialist guidance):**
- Fosfomycin 3 g granules single dose (lower efficacy than 5-day nitrofurantoin)

**If no improvement in 48 hours:** Send MSU; consider resistance; reassess diagnosis.

### 2.2 UTI in Men

- Send midstream urine (MSU) for culture before treating
- Treat as complicated UTI: **trimethoprim 200 mg BD for 14 days** or **nitrofurantoin 100 mg MR BD for 7 days**
- Consider prostatitis if febrile or perineal discomfort — fluoroquinolone (ciprofloxacin 500 mg BD for 28 days) after urology advice

### 2.3 Catheter-Associated UTI

- **Do not treat asymptomatic bacteriuria** in catheterised patients
- Treat symptomatic CAUTI: change catheter if possible; send CSU; treat for 7 days
- Empirical: trimethoprim 200 mg BD or co-amoxiclav 625 mg TDS

## 3. Upper Respiratory Tract Infections (URTI)

### 3.1 Sore Throat / Tonsillitis

- **Antibiotics NOT recommended** for most sore throats (80–90% viral)
- Use **FeverPAIN or Centor score** to stratify:
  - FeverPAIN 0–1 or Centor 0–2: **No antibiotic**; self-care advice
  - FeverPAIN 2–3: **Delayed prescription** (back-up prescription)
  - FeverPAIN 4–5 or Centor ≥3 with systemic upset: **Immediate antibiotic**

**First-line (if antibiotic indicated):**
- **Phenoxymethylpenicillin (penicillin V)** 500 mg QDS for **5–10 days**

**Penicillin allergy (non-severe):**
- **Clarithromycin** 500 mg BD for 5 days
- **Erythromycin** 500 mg QDS for 5 days (preferred in pregnancy)

**Do not prescribe amoxicillin for sore throat** — risk of widespread rash if infectious mononucleosis (EBV)

### 3.2 Acute Otitis Media (AOM)

- **Most cases resolve without antibiotics** (60% resolve in 24 hours without treatment)
- Immediate antibiotic if: <2 years with bilateral AOM; any age with discharge; systemic illness
- **Amoxicillin** 500 mg TDS for **5 days** (children: 125–250 mg TDS)
- Penicillin allergy: **Clarithromycin** 250–500 mg BD for 5 days

### 3.3 Acute Sinusitis

- **Antibiotics NOT recommended** if symptoms <10 days (likely viral)
- Consider antibiotic if symptoms >10 days, unilateral facial/tooth pain, or worsening after initial improvement

**First-line:** **Amoxicillin** 500 mg TDS for **5 days**
**Penicillin allergy:** Doxycycline 200 mg stat then 100 mg OD for 4 days (total 5 days)

## 4. Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP)

### Severity Assessment (CURB-65 Score):
- Confusion: 1 point
- Urea >7 mmol/L: 1 point
- Respiratory rate ≥30/min: 1 point
- BP <90/60 mmHg: 1 point
- Age ≥65: 1 point

**Score 0–1 (low severity — home treatment):**
- **Amoxicillin** 500 mg TDS for **5 days**
- Penicillin allergy: **Doxycycline** 200 mg on day 1, then 100 mg OD for 4 days (total 5 days)

**Score 2 (moderate severity — consider hospital):**
- Amoxicillin 500 mg TDS + Clarithromycin 500 mg BD for 5–7 days (atypical cover)

**Score ≥3 (high severity — hospital admission required):**
- IV Co-amoxiclav 1.2 g TDS + IV Clarithromycin 500 mg BD
- De-escalate to oral at 48–72 hours if clinical improvement

**Atypical CAP (Mycoplasma, Legionella, Chlamydophila):**
- Doxycycline 200 mg OD or clarithromycin 500 mg BD

## 5. Skin and Soft Tissue Infections

### 5.1 Cellulitis (Eron Classification)

**Class I (no systemic illness, no co-morbidities):** Oral antibiotics; no hospital admission needed
- **Flucloxacillin** 500 mg–1 g QDS for **5–7 days** (first-line for Staphylococcus/Streptococcus)
- Penicillin allergy: **Doxycycline** 200 mg OD or **Clarithromycin** 500 mg BD

**Class II–IV (systemic illness or significant co-morbidities):** IV antibiotics; consider hospital
- IV Flucloxacillin 1–2 g QDS ± Benzylpenicillin 1.2 g QDS

**MRSA confirmed or suspected:** Seek microbiology advice
- **Doxycycline** 100 mg BD (community MRSA, sensitive)
- **Trimethoprim** 200 mg BD
- If systemic: Vancomycin (hospital) or linezolid (specialist only)

### 5.2 Impetigo

**Localised (< 3 lesions):**
- **Hydrogen peroxide 1% cream** — topical; apply TDS for 5 days (first-line)

**More extensive or unresponsive:**
- **Topical fusidic acid** 2% BD for 5 days (limit use to prevent resistance)
- Oral: **Flucloxacillin** 500 mg QDS for 5–7 days

### 5.3 Wound Infections (Post-operative / Bites)

- Human/animal bites: **Co-amoxiclav** 625 mg TDS for 5–7 days
- Penicillin allergy: **Metronidazole** 400 mg TDS + **Doxycycline** 100 mg BD for 5–7 days

## 6. Allergy Documentation Requirements

All prescriptions must include documented allergy status:
- **No known drug allergies (NKDA):** Document explicitly
- **Penicillin allergy:** Record reaction type — anaphylaxis/angioedema (severe); rash without systemic features (mild/moderate)
  - Mild penicillin reaction: Co-amoxiclav often safe (5% cross-reactivity with cephalosporins; 1% with carbapenems)
  - Severe penicillin reaction (anaphylaxis): Avoid all beta-lactams; consider delabelling referral
- **Delabelling:** Refer patients with documented penicillin allergy for allergy testing — majority (>80%) are not truly allergic

## 7. Duration Principles

| Infection | Typical Duration |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Uncomplicated UTI (women) | 5 days (nitrofurantoin) / 7 days (trimethoprim) |
| UTI in men | 14 days |
| Sore throat | 5–10 days |
| CAP (mild-moderate) | 5 days |
| Cellulitis (Class I) | 5–7 days (review at day 3) |
| Impetigo (oral) | 5–7 days |
| Sinusitis (if treating) | 5 days |

---

## 8. Culture Requirements

Send cultures before treatment in:
- Recurrent infections (≥2 episodes/year)
- Treatment failure at 48–72 hours
- All hospital-acquired infections
- Immunocompromised patients
- Suspected MRSA
- Severe sepsis (blood cultures x2 before first antibiotic dose)
- UTI in men, pregnant women, or catheterised patients
