# Mental Health Screening and Management Guidelines
**Version:** 2024 | **Source:** NICE CG90, CG123, NG222 / IAPT Handbook | **Category:** mental_health, psychiatry, general

---

## 1. Screening Tools and Interpretation

### 1.1 PHQ-9 — Depression Screening

The Patient Health Questionnaire-9 (PHQ-9) scores 9 items on a 0–3 scale (total 0–27).

| Score | Severity | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------|--------------------|
| 0–4 | Minimal / No depression | Watchful waiting; self-care advice |
| 5–9 | Mild depression | Active monitoring; consider guided self-help |
| 10–14 | Moderate depression | Structured psychological intervention (CBT); consider SSRI |
| 15–19 | Moderately severe depression | SSRI plus high-intensity psychological therapy |
| 20–27 | Severe depression | Urgent review; combination treatment; consider secondary care referral |

**PHQ-9 Question 9 (suicidal ideation):** Any score >0 requires immediate risk assessment regardless of total score.

### 1.2 GAD-7 — Generalised Anxiety Disorder

The Generalised Anxiety Disorder-7 (GAD-7) scores 7 items on a 0–3 scale (total 0–21).

| Score | Severity |
|-------|----------|
| 0–4 | Minimal anxiety |
| 5–9 | Mild anxiety |
| 10–14 | Moderate anxiety |
| 15–21 | Severe anxiety |

**Threshold for intervention:** GAD-7 ≥8 — offer psychological or pharmacological treatment.

### 1.3 Other Validated Tools

- **PHQ-2:** Two-question depression pre-screen; score ≥3 proceed to PHQ-9
- **AUDIT-C:** Alcohol use screening — use in all mental health assessments
- **AUDIT-10:** Full alcohol dependence assessment if AUDIT-C ≥5
- **Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS):** Use for structured suicide risk assessment
- **DASS-21:** Depression, anxiety and stress — useful in primary care mental health
- **EPIC:** Early Psychosis Indicator Checklist — consider if prodromal psychosis suspected

## 2. Initial Assessment

At first presentation with mental health concerns:
- Biopsychosocial assessment (precipitating, perpetuating, protective factors)
- PHQ-9 and GAD-7 scoring
- Risk assessment (suicidal ideation, self-harm, harm to others)
- Safeguarding screen (domestic violence — DASH checklist; child protection concerns)
- Physical health review (rule out organic causes: thyroid dysfunction, anaemia, medications)
- Medication and substance use history
- Social circumstances, housing, employment, social support network
- Previous mental health history, previous treatments, response to treatment

## 3. Stepped Care Model

### Step 1 — Active Monitoring (PHQ-9 0–9, mild depression)

- Psychoeducation about depression and anxiety
- Self-help resources: mood diaries, bibliotherapy, exercise prescription (≥3 sessions/week)
- Online digital CBT (e.g., SilverCloud, Beating the Blues, BfB Labs)
- Social prescribing referral (wellbeing activities, peer support groups)
- **Review at 2–4 weeks**

### Step 2 — Low-Intensity Psychological Interventions (PHQ-9 5–14, mild-moderate)

- **IAPT (Improving Access to Psychological Therapies) referral**
- Guided self-help based on CBT principles (6–8 sessions, 90 minutes total contact time)
- Behavioural activation for depression
- Applied relaxation for anxiety
- Mindfulness-based cognitive therapy (MBCT) — particularly for recurrent depression
- Group CBT programmes

### Step 3 — High-Intensity Psychological Treatment (PHQ-9 ≥10, moderate-severe)

- **Individual CBT:** 12–20 sessions, one session per week (50–60 minutes)
- **Interpersonal Therapy (IPT):** 16–20 sessions — effective for depression related to grief/role transitions
- **Behavioural Activation (BA):** 16–20 sessions — evidence-based for severe depression
- **EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitisation and Reprocessing):** First-line for PTSD

## 4. Pharmacological Treatment

### 4.1 First-Line Antidepressant: SSRIs

**Sertraline** is the preferred first-line SSRI due to evidence base, tolerability, and low interaction profile:
- **Starting dose:** 50 mg OD (morning)
- **Review at 2 weeks** for side effects and adherence; **review at 4 weeks** for efficacy
- If partial response at 4 weeks: **titrate to 100 mg OD**
- Titrate further every **4 weeks** by 50 mg increments
- **Maximum dose:** 200 mg OD
- Usual therapeutic dose: 100–200 mg OD for moderate-severe depression

**Alternative SSRIs:**
- **Fluoxetine** 20 mg OD (max 60 mg OD) — longer half-life; safer in overdose; preferred in young people
- **Citalopram** 20 mg OD (max 40 mg OD; 20 mg OD in >65 years or hepatic impairment) — QTc prolongation risk
- **Escitalopram** 10 mg OD (max 20 mg OD; 10 mg max in >65 years)

### 4.2 SSRI Side Effect Profile and Management

**Common early side effects (usually transient, first 1–2 weeks):**
- Nausea, headache, agitation, insomnia
- Advise patients: effects are temporary; take with food; do not stop abruptly

**Ongoing side effects:**
- Sexual dysfunction (30–40%): dose reduction, drug holiday, consider mirtazapine or bupropion
- Weight gain (especially paroxetine): switch to sertraline or fluoxetine
- Serotonin syndrome: rare but serious — headache, diarrhoea, sweating, hyperthermia, myoclonus; withdraw drug immediately

**Discontinuation syndrome:** Taper dose over 4+ weeks when stopping (especially paroxetine, venlafaxine). Do NOT stop abruptly.

### 4.3 Second-Line Agents (if SSRI ineffective or not tolerated)

- **Mirtazapine** 15 mg nocte (increase to 30–45 mg) — sedating; useful for insomnia and poor appetite; weight gain
- **Venlafaxine** (SNRI) 75 mg OD (increase to max 225 mg in divided doses) — effective for anxiety comorbidity; monitor BP
- **Duloxetine** 60 mg OD — useful for pain comorbidity; avoid in hepatic impairment

### 4.4 Augmentation Strategies (partial responders — specialist involvement recommended)

- Add lithium to antidepressant (specialist prescribing; requires regular lithium levels, renal and thyroid monitoring)
- Add atypical antipsychotic: quetiapine 50–300 mg OD; aripiprazole 5–15 mg OD
- Add buspirone 5–10 mg BD–TDS for anxiety augmentation

## 5. Monitoring Schedule

| Time Point | Action |
|------------|--------|
| 2 weeks post-initiation | Review side effects; reinforce adherence; assess suicidal ideation (PHQ-9 Q9) |
| 4 weeks post-initiation | Assess treatment response (PHQ-9); titrate dose if partial response |
| 8 weeks post-initiation | Full response assessment; if no response, review diagnosis, consider switch |
| 3 months | PHQ-9 repeat; discuss continuation of treatment (minimum 6 months after remission) |
| 6 months | Consider step-down; plan discontinuation if stable |

**PHQ-9 response criteria:**
- Response: ≥50% reduction in PHQ-9 score from baseline
- Remission: PHQ-9 <5
- Non-response: <25% reduction at 4 weeks — review treatment

## 6. Crisis Assessment and Safeguarding

### 6.1 Risk Stratification

**Low risk:** Passive thoughts of death ("I wish I were dead"); no intent or plan; protective factors present (family, future goals)
**Moderate risk:** Active suicidal ideation with some intent; no specific plan; agrees to safety plan
**High risk / Imminent:** Specific plan; access to means; prior attempt; intent to act

### 6.2 Safety Planning (for moderate risk)

Document a **CRISIS plan** in notes:
- **C**oping strategies the patient will use (distraction, mindfulness, calling a friend)
- **R**emove means (safe storage of medications; limit access to sharp objects)
- **I**dentify trusted contacts (family member, friend, nominated supporter)
- **S**upport services (Samaritans 116 123; Crisis line; emergency contacts)
- **I**ntervention plan (what patient agrees to do if at immediate risk)
- **S**ign-off — document date of plan; countersigned by clinician

### 6.3 Immediate Actions for High Risk

- **Do not leave patient alone**
- Contact mental health crisis team (CRHT) for same-day assessment
- If patient refuses and risk is imminent: call 999; consider Section 136 (Police and Mental Health Act 1983)
- Safeguarding alert if dependent children or vulnerable adults in household

---

## 7. Referral to Secondary Care (CMHT / Psychiatry)

Refer to Community Mental Health Team (CMHT) if:
- PHQ-9 ≥20 with inadequate response to first-line treatment
- Two failed antidepressant trials
- Bipolar disorder suspected (hypomania, rapid cycling, mixed states)
- Psychotic features (command hallucinations, nihilistic delusions)
- High suicide risk
- Eating disorder with physical risk (BMI <15, electrolyte abnormalities)
- First-episode psychosis — urgent referral to Early Intervention in Psychosis (EIP) team within 2 weeks
- Complex PTSD requiring specialist trauma therapy
- Personality disorder with recurrent self-harm

## 8. Special Populations

**Children and young people (<18 years):**
- Fluoxetine is the only SSRI licensed for depression in under-18s
- Psychological therapy first-line; medication only if psychological therapy fails
- Refer to CAMHS (Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services) for moderate-severe cases

**Perinatal mental health (pregnancy and postpartum):**
- Avoid paroxetine (cardiac defects in first trimester; neonatal withdrawal)
- Sertraline and fluoxetine: relatively safe (discuss risk:benefit)
- Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) — score ≥13 requires urgent review
- Refer to specialist Perinatal Mental Health Team if moderate-severe illness

**Older adults (≥65 years):**
- Start at half the adult dose; titrate more slowly
- Citalopram max 20 mg OD (QTc prolongation)
- SSRI + falls risk: sodium monitoring; consider SNRI or mirtazapine
