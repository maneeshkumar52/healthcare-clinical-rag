# Type 2 Diabetes Management Guidelines
**Version:** 2024 | **Source:** NICE NG28 / ADA Standards of Care | **Category:** general, primary_care

---

## 1. Diagnosis Criteria

Type 2 diabetes is confirmed by any ONE of the following on two separate occasions (or once with symptoms):

- **HbA1c:** ≥48 mmol/mol (≥6.5%) — preferred diagnostic test
- **Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG):** ≥7.0 mmol/L (fasting = no caloric intake for ≥8 hours)
- **2-hour Plasma Glucose:** ≥11.1 mmol/L during 75g oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT)
- **Random Plasma Glucose:** ≥11.1 mmol/L with classic hyperglycaemia symptoms

**Pre-diabetes thresholds:**
- HbA1c 42–47 mmol/mol (6.0–6.4%) — impaired glucose regulation
- FPG 6.1–6.9 mmol/L — impaired fasting glucose

---

## 2. Initial Assessment

At diagnosis, perform:
- Full blood count, renal function (eGFR, creatinine), liver function tests
- Urine albumin:creatinine ratio (ACR) — screen for nephropathy
- Fasting lipid profile
- Blood pressure measurement
- BMI and waist circumference
- Foot examination (10g monofilament, ankle-brachial pressure index if indicated)
- Retinal screening referral

---

## 3. Lifestyle Intervention (First-line for all patients)

- **Structured diabetes education:** Offer DESMOND programme or equivalent within 12 months of diagnosis
- **Weight loss target:** ≥5% body weight reduction; consider very-low-calorie diet (800 kcal/day) for BMI >27
- **Physical activity:** ≥150 minutes moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week; resistance exercise 2–3 times/week
- **Dietary advice:** Low glycaemic index foods, reduced saturated fat, limit refined carbohydrates, Mediterranean-style diet
- **Smoking cessation:** Refer to NHS Stop Smoking Services
- **Alcohol:** Limit to ≤14 units/week

---

## 4. Pharmacological Treatment

### 4.1 First-Line: Metformin

**Indication:** Initiate if HbA1c remains ≥48 mmol/mol after 3 months of lifestyle intervention (or sooner if HbA1c ≥58 mmol/mol).

**Dosing (standard-release):**
- Start: 500 mg once daily with food (evening meal)
- Titrate: Increase by 500 mg every 1–2 weeks as tolerated
- Target dose: 1 g twice daily (BD) with meals
- Maximum dose: 1 g three times daily (TDS) if tolerated

**Modified-release metformin:** Consider if GI side effects limit standard-release; same dosing schedule.

**Contraindications / Dose Adjustments:**
- eGFR 45–59 mL/min/1.73m²: Continue; review regularly
- eGFR 30–44 mL/min/1.73m²: Reduce maximum dose to 500 mg BD; increased lactic acidosis risk
- eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m²: **Contraindicated** — stop metformin
- Acute illness, surgery, iodinated contrast: Withhold temporarily (48 hours post-contrast)
- Hepatic impairment (ALT >3× ULN): Avoid

### 4.2 Second-Line Options (add to Metformin or use if Metformin contraindicated)

**SGLT2 Inhibitors** (preferred if established cardiovascular disease, heart failure, or CKD):
- Empagliflozin 10 mg once daily (increase to 25 mg if tolerated)
- Dapagliflozin 10 mg once daily
- Canagliflozin 100 mg once daily (with first meal)
- **Do not use if eGFR <45 mL/min/1.73m² (glucose-lowering effect reduced)**
- Caution: diabetic ketoacidosis risk (including euglycaemic DKA); genital mycotic infections

**GLP-1 Receptor Agonists** (preferred if BMI >35, or cardiovascular risk):
- Semaglutide 0.5 mg SC weekly (titrate to 1 mg after 4 weeks)
- Dulaglutide 0.75 mg SC weekly (titrate to 1.5 mg after 4 weeks)
- Liraglutide 0.6 mg SC OD (titrate by 0.6 mg every week to 1.8 mg)
- **Contraindicated** in personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2

**DPP-4 Inhibitors** (well-tolerated, weight neutral):
- Sitagliptin 100 mg OD (reduce to 50 mg if eGFR 30–59; 25 mg if eGFR <30)
- Alogliptin 25 mg OD

**Sulfonylureas** (avoid if hypoglycaemia risk is high):
- Gliclazide MR 30–120 mg OD — preferred sulfonylurea
- Risk of hypoglycaemia; weight gain

## 5. Monitoring

### HbA1c Targets:
- General target: **<53 mmol/mol (<7.0%)**
- Less stringent (<58 mmol/mol) if elderly, frailty, limited life expectancy, or hypoglycaemia risk
- Tighter target (<48 mmol/mol) if newly diagnosed and managed by lifestyle/metformin alone

### Monitoring Frequency:
- HbA1c: Every **3 months** until stable (2 consecutive results within target)
- Then every **6 months** if stable
- eGFR and electrolytes: **Annually** (or 6-monthly if CKD)
- Urine ACR: **Annually**
- Fasting lipids: **Annually**
- Blood pressure: **Every visit** (at least annually)
- Foot review: **Annually** (low risk); 3–6 monthly (moderate/high risk)
- Retinal screening: **Annually**

---

## 6. Blood Pressure Management

- **General target:** <140/90 mmHg
- **If CKD (ACR >3 mg/mmol) or established cardiovascular disease:** <130/80 mmHg
- First-line antihypertensive: ACE inhibitor (or ARB if ACE-intolerant) — reno-protective
- If ACR >30 mg/mmol, start ACE inhibitor regardless of blood pressure

---

## 7. Hypoglycaemia Management

**Mild-Moderate (conscious patient):**
- 15–20 g fast-acting glucose (e.g., 150–200 mL fruit juice, 4–6 glucose tablets, or Glucogel)
- Recheck glucose after 15 minutes; repeat if still <4.0 mmol/L
- Follow with long-acting carbohydrate (e.g., biscuits, bread) once glucose ≥4.0 mmol/L

**Severe (impaired consciousness):**
- Glucagon 1 mg IM/SC (if available) or IV 10% glucose 100–200 mL
- Once conscious: oral carbohydrate as above
- **Do not give oral glucose to unconscious patient**

---

## 8. Driving and Diabetes

- DVLA notification required for insulin-treated drivers
- Check glucose before driving and every 2 hours on long journeys
- Minimum glucose for driving: ≥5.0 mmol/L
- If glucose <5.0 mmol/L: treat and wait 45 minutes before driving

---

## 9. Sick Day Rules

Advise patients to:
- Continue diabetes medications unless vomiting prevents oral intake
- Increase glucose monitoring frequency (every 2–4 hours)
- Maintain hydration
- Seek medical review if vomiting, unable to eat, glucose consistently >17 mmol/L, or ketones present

---

## 10. Referral Criteria

Refer to diabetes specialist team if:
- HbA1c remains ≥75 mmol/mol despite dual/triple therapy
- Consideration of insulin therapy
- eGFR declining rapidly (>5 mL/min/year)
- Recurrent severe hypoglycaemia
- Suspected MODY (maturity-onset diabetes of the young) — family history, young age at diagnosis
- Pregnancy or planning pregnancy
