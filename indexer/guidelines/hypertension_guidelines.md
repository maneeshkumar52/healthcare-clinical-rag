# Hypertension Management Guidelines
**Version:** 2024 | **Source:** NICE NG136 / BHS Guidelines | **Category:** general, primary_care, cardiac

---

## 1. Definition and Classification

**Clinic Blood Pressure Thresholds:**

| Stage | Clinic Systolic | Clinic Diastolic |
|-------|-----------------|------------------|
| Stage 1 Hypertension | 140–159 mmHg | 90–99 mmHg |
| Stage 2 Hypertension | ≥160 mmHg | ≥100 mmHg |
| Severe Hypertension | ≥180 mmHg | ≥120 mmHg |
| Normal | <130 mmHg | <85 mmHg |
| High-Normal (pre-hypertension) | 130–139 mmHg | 85–89 mmHg |

**Hypertensive urgency/emergency:**
- SBP ≥180 or DBP ≥120 mmHg with signs of end-organ damage (encephalopathy, chest pain, acute kidney injury): urgent same-day specialist referral

---

## 2. Diagnosis Confirmation

**Ambulatory Blood Pressure Monitoring (ABPM) is required** to confirm diagnosis if clinic BP is ≥140/90 mmHg:
- ABPM daytime average ≥135/85 mmHg confirms Stage 1 hypertension
- ABPM daytime average ≥150/95 mmHg confirms Stage 2 hypertension
- ABPM is preferred to Home Blood Pressure Monitoring (HBPM) where possible

**HBPM alternative:** Two measurements twice daily for 4–7 days; discard first day readings; average remainder.
- HBPM average ≥135/85 mmHg confirms Stage 1

---

## 3. Initial Investigation

All newly diagnosed hypertensive patients:
- Urine dipstick (protein, blood, glucose)
- Urine albumin:creatinine ratio (ACR)
- Blood: eGFR, electrolytes (K+), fasting glucose, HbA1c, fasting lipids, full blood count
- 12-lead ECG (look for LVH, ischaemia)
- Fundoscopy if severe hypertension or end-organ damage suspected
- Assess 10-year cardiovascular risk (QRISK3)

---

## 4. Lifestyle Modifications (All patients, concurrent with medication)

- **Weight reduction:** Target BMI <25 kg/m²; each 1 kg weight loss reduces SBP by ~1 mmHg
- **Dietary sodium:** Reduce to <6 g/day (2.4 g sodium); DASH diet recommended
- **Alcohol:** Limit to ≤14 units/week; avoid binge drinking
- **Physical activity:** ≥150 minutes moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week (e.g., brisk walking, cycling); isometric exercise (hand-grip) may have additional benefit
- **DASH Diet:** Increase fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy; reduce saturated fat and red meat
- **Caffeine:** Reduce excessive intake; >4 cups/day associated with BP elevation
- **Smoking cessation:** Does not lower BP directly but essential for cardiovascular risk reduction

---

## 5. Pharmacological Treatment Algorithm

### Step 1 — First-Line Monotherapy

**Patients aged <55 years (non-Black ethnicity):**
- **ACE inhibitor (ACEI):** Ramipril 2.5 mg OD, titrate to 5–10 mg OD over 4–8 weeks
- Alternative ACEI: Perindopril 4 mg OD, titrate to 8 mg OD; lisinopril 10 mg OD, titrate to 40 mg OD
- If ACEI not tolerated (persistent dry cough, angioedema): **ARB** — Losartan 50 mg OD (max 100 mg OD); candesartan 8 mg OD (max 32 mg OD)

**Patients aged ≥55 years OR Black ethnicity (any age):**
- **Calcium Channel Blocker (CCB):** Amlodipine 5 mg OD, titrate to 10 mg OD
- Alternative CCB: Felodipine 5 mg OD; lercanidipine 10 mg OD
- If CCB not tolerated (ankle oedema): Thiazide-like diuretic — Indapamide 2.5 mg OD (preferred over bendroflumethiazide)

---

### Step 2 — Combination Therapy (if BP target not reached after 4 weeks)

**Add second agent to Step 1:**
- ACEI/ARB + CCB (preferred combination)
- If CCB not tolerated or patient has oedema/heart failure: ACEI/ARB + Thiazide-like diuretic (indapamide 2.5 mg OD)

---

### Step 3 — Triple Therapy

- ACEI/ARB + CCB + Thiazide-like diuretic
- Reassess adherence before adding fourth agent

---

### Step 4 — Resistant Hypertension (BP not controlled on optimal Step 3 therapy)

- Check for secondary causes (renal artery stenosis, primary aldosteronism, phaeochromocytoma)
- If K+ <4.5 mmol/L: **Add spironolactone 25 mg OD** (monitor K+ and eGFR at 4 weeks)
- If K+ ≥4.5 mmol/L: Add alpha-blocker (doxazosin 4 mg OD, titrate to 16 mg OD) or beta-blocker (bisoprolol 5 mg OD)
- Refer to hypertension specialist

---

## 6. Blood Pressure Targets

| Patient Group | Target |
|---------------|--------|
| Age <80 years (general) | <140/90 mmHg (clinic); <135/85 mmHg (ABPM) |
| Age ≥80 years | <150/90 mmHg (clinic) |
| Diabetes mellitus | <130/80 mmHg |
| CKD (ACR >70 mg/mmol or eGFR <30) | <130/80 mmHg |
| Established cardiovascular disease | <130/80 mmHg |
| Post-stroke / TIA | <130/80 mmHg |

---

## 7. Monitoring Schedule

- **Newly initiated or dose changed:** Review blood pressure and side effects at **4 weeks**
- **Stable patients:** Review every **6–12 months** (annually for low-risk stable patients)
- **eGFR and electrolytes:** 4 weeks after starting ACEI/ARB or spironolactone, then **annually**
- **After Step 4 addition of spironolactone:** Monitor K+ and eGFR at 1 month, 3 months, then 6-monthly

---

## 8. Drug-Specific Contraindications and Cautions

### ACE Inhibitors / ARBs
- **Absolute contraindications:** Previous angioedema with ACEI; bilateral renal artery stenosis; pregnancy (all trimesters) — teratogenic
- **Cautions:** eGFR <30 mL/min (can use with monitoring); hyperkalaemia (K+ >5.0 mmol/L)
- **Do not combine ACEI + ARB** (dual renin-angiotensin blockade — increased risk of AKI and hyperkalaemia)

### Calcium Channel Blockers (dihydropyridines — amlodipine, felodipine)
- **Cautions:** Ankle oedema (switch to non-dihydropyridine or reduce dose); grapefruit juice interaction
- **Avoid** in unstable angina (short-acting nifedipine); severe aortic stenosis

### Thiazide-like diuretics (indapamide)
- **Cautions:** Hyponatraemia (elderly); hypokalaemia (monitor K+); gout exacerbation
- **Avoid** in severe renal impairment (eGFR <30 mL/min — ineffective)

### Spironolactone
- **Contraindications:** K+ ≥5.0 mmol/L; eGFR <30 mL/min; Addison's disease
- **Cautions:** Gynaecomastia in males (switch to eplerenone if troublesome)

---

## 9. Secondary Causes of Hypertension

Consider investigation if:
- Resistant hypertension despite three optimal drugs
- Onset before age 40 (especially in non-obese patients)
- Accelerated/malignant hypertension
- Hypokalaemia without diuretic use
- Episodic hypertension with headache/sweating/palpitations

**Investigations:** Aldosterone:renin ratio; renal ultrasound (Doppler); 24-hour urine catecholamines/metanephrines; renal artery angiography if indicated.

---

## 10. Special Populations

**Pregnancy:** Alpha-methyldopa 250 mg BD–TDS (safe in all trimesters); labetalol 100–400 mg BD; nifedipine SR
**CKD with proteinuria:** ACEI or ARB mandatory (reno-protective); target <130/80 mmHg
**Elderly (≥80 years):** Initiate at lower doses; target <150/90 mmHg; beware orthostatic hypotension
